Visiting a Church for the First Time in Chula Vista: What to Expect

By the God's House of Rest team Reviewed by Pastor R. Tyrone Robertson Updated June 18, 2026 6 min read

Here's the short answer: walking into a church for the first time should feel welcoming, not intimidating. At God's House of Rest in Chula Vista, you can wear what's comfortable, arrive 10–15 minutes early, expect a service of about 90 minutes, and you'll never be singled out or pressured. Below is everything else you might be wondering — answered plainly.

Quick Answer
  • What to wear: Anything comfortable — there's no dress code.
  • When to arrive: 10–15 minutes early to park and get settled.
  • How long: About 90 minutes; the message is ~30–40 minutes.
  • Kids: Children's Church (ages 5–12) runs during the service.
  • Pressure: None. Observe at your own pace; giving is never expected of guests.

"Come to Me, all you who labor and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest."

— Matthew 11:28 (NKJV)

Why the first visit feels so big (and why it doesn't have to)

If you've been thinking about visiting a church but keep putting it off, you're not alone. For most people the hesitation isn't about faith — it's about the unknown. Where do I park? What if I sit in the wrong place? What if someone asks me a question I can't answer? Those worries are completely normal, and a good church works hard to remove every one of them before you arrive.

Our name says what we're about: rest. We take the words of Jesus in Matthew 11:28 — "Come to Me, all you who labor and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest" — as a literal invitation. You don't have to have it together to walk through the door. You just have to come.

What should I wear to church?

Wear whatever is comfortable and respectful. You'll see people in jeans and sneakers sitting right next to people in their Sunday best, and nobody thinks twice about it. If dressing up helps you feel ready for worship, do it. If you'd rather come casual, that's just as welcome. The point is for you to focus on what's happening — not on your outfit.

When should I arrive?

Aim to arrive about 10 to 15 minutes early. That window lets you park (free parking is in the shopping-center lot at 618 E Street), find the entrance at Suite H, and get greeted without feeling rushed. Our welcome team will be outside, and you're encouraged to tell them it's your first time — they'll point you to everything you need, including where to take your kids.

What actually happens during the service?

A typical Sunday at God's House of Rest looks like this:

  • Worship music to open — you can sing along, hum, or simply listen.
  • The message — a practical, scripture-based teaching, usually 30–40 minutes. You can browse past messages on our sermons page to get a feel before you come.
  • Prayer — our prayer team is available after every service if you'd like someone to pray with you.

If communion or a baptism is part of that Sunday, it will always be explained from the front so no one is left guessing. When in doubt, you can simply follow along or stay seated.

You don't have to have it together to walk through the door. You just have to come.

Will I be put on the spot?

No — and we mean it. We will never ask you to stand up in front of the room, wear a "visitor" badge, or explain why you're there. Giving is for those who call this church home; it is never expected of guests. You are free to participate as much or as little as you want.

Where do my kids go?

Our Children's Church runs at the same time as the main service for kids ages 5–12, in a safe, secure, and genuinely fun environment with vetted volunteers. Teens have Teen Vibe Ministry, and you're always welcome to keep little ones with you if you'd prefer.

What happens after the service?

Stay as long or as little as you like. If something in the message stirred a question, our team and pastors would love to talk. If you'd rather slip out quietly and think it over, that's completely fine too. There's no follow-up pressure — just an open door whenever you're ready to come back.

How to plan your first visit

The easiest way to take the nervousness out of your first Sunday is to let us know you're coming. Send us a quick message or use our Plan Your Visit page, and we'll have someone ready to greet you by name. Service is Sundays at 10:00 AM at 618 E Street, Suite H, Chula Vista, CA 91910.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I wear to church for the first time?

Wear whatever is comfortable and respectful. There's no dress code — people come in everything from jeans to Sunday best. The goal is for you to focus on the service, not your outfit.

How early should I arrive?

About 10 to 15 minutes early. That gives you time to park, find the entrance, get greeted, and settle in without feeling rushed.

How long is the service?

Our Sunday worship runs about 90 minutes to two hours, including music, the message, and prayer. The sermon itself is typically 30–40 minutes.

Will I be singled out as a visitor?

No. We never put guests on the spot or pressure you to give. You participate only at your own comfort level.

Where do my kids go during the service?

Children's Church runs at the same time as the main service for ages 5–12 in a safe, fun environment. You're also welcome to keep your children with you.
